London's Hidden Parks

Hampstead Heath Pergola

Offerring stunning views over the heath, Hampstead Heath's Pergola is a much underated gem situated within the Heath. Constructed in the early 1900's the Pergola is the product of a rich mans love of warm summer evenings. Definitely a must see for anyone visiting North London!

Ravenscourt Park Walled Garden

Ravenscourt Park is a lovely open space near Hammersmith in West London, tended to by volunteers and supported by neighbours.

St Dunstan in the East

Located between Monument and the Tower of London is St Dunstan in the East, a bombed-out church in the City. Offering a secluded lunch time spot in the heart of the city.

Woodbury Wetlands

Woodberry Wetlands is a nature reserve and designated Site of Metropolitan Importance on the site of the East Reservoir in Stoke Newington. Only opened in 2016, it offers spetcacular views across the former water processing site.

The Rookery, Streatham

With a lovely cafe, the Rookery is an attractive, landscaped area with an ornamental pond, flower and herbaceous beds, and a rock garden with streams.

Christchurch Greyfriars

The ruins of an old church, this hidden site is a perfect place to relax within the heart of the city.

Abney Park Cemetery, Stoke Newington

Abney Park is one of the magnificant 7 cemetrys in London. It offers a forest like walk within the middle of Hackney.

Kyoto Garden, Kensington

A lovely hidden garden with colourful plants, shrubs and water features, created by an eminent Japanese designer.
